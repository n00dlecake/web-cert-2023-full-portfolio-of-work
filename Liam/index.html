Biography

Born in Fukushima, Japan. In 1961 he attended Bunka Fashion College in Tokyo, graduating in 1984. His journey began at CDG(Comme des Gar√ßons) as a patternmaker, later becoming the chief designer of the Tricot knitwear line in 1987 later transitioning to the CDG Homme line, he eventually staarted his own name collection in 1992, showcasing in Tokyo. In 1993, he established "Junya Watanabe CDG", expanding to Paris for womanswear and introducing a mens line in 2000. Notable collaborations include Converse, Levis, Nike and The North Face. His diverse projects range from a Tamiya toy car to a solar-powered jacket. In 2020, he launched a capsule collection with Brooks Brothers and collabrated on a cosplay collection with CDG AND Yoko Taro. Currently based in Tokyo, he continus his work at CDG. Watanabe frequently employs the term monozukuri to describe his creations. Similar to his mentor Rei Kawakubo, Watanabe is renowned for his innovative and distinctive clothing designs. He has a particular interest in synthetic and technologically advanced textiles, as seen in his spring/summer 2001 line, but also incorporates traditional materials like cotton in his spring/summer 2003 collection. Influences from Pierre Cardin and Issey Miyake can also be observed in his work. Watanabe is often recognized as a "techno couture" designer, known for crafting uniquely structured garments using modern, technical materials. However, his 2015 show in Paris faced criticism for inappropriate cultural appropriation. Compared to his mentor Rei Kawakubo, Watanabe's work is considered more practical. He collaborates with a team of 30 individuals and relies on an English translator.
